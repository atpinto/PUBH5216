Module 1 Activity: Trial Design
Question 1
You are providing advice on trial design to a clinician wishing to determine whether dietary supplementation with B vitamins, or omega 3 fatty acids, or both could prevent major cardiovascular events in patients with a history of ischaemic heart disease or stroke.

(a) What type(s) of trial design do you think might be reasonable? What information would you seek from the clinician to better inform your recommendation regarding the optimal design?

(b) What methodological features could be incorporated in the trial design to avoid possible biases? Describe the role of these features and how they will be implemented in practice?

Question 2
Your help has been sought to design a trial to assess the effectiveness of a class-room based physical activity program, versus the standard class program, on physical and psychological health in young schoolchildren. The nine-month physical activity program comprises daily physical education lessons and short activity breaks, as well as physical activity ‘homework’.

(a) What type(s) of trial design do you think might be reasonable? What information would you seek from the clinician to better inform your recommendation regarding the optimal design?

(b) Suggest a primary outcome for the trial. When and how could this be measured? What other information would you recommend collecting as part of trial procedures? When and how should this be collected?

(c) What considerations may be relevant when:

i. Selecting schools for potential inclusion in the trial?

ii. Determining the eligibility of children to participate in the trial?
